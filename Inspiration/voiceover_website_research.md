# Voiceover Artist Website Research

Research into existing voiceover artist websites to inform the design of Maud Fasel's multilingual voiceover website. Sites reviewed include: leoniedoesvoices.com, alteregovoices.com (Cromerty York), voicebymaria.com (Maria Pendolino), liliwexuvoiceovers.com (Lili Wexu), rupertdegas.com, neilwilliamsvoiceover.com, plus analysis articles from sitebuilderreport.com, htmlburger.com, squarestash.com, and voice123.com.

---

## 1. Overall Structure & Navigation

Almost all professional voiceover sites follow a consistent navigation structure:

- **Home** — hero section + quick demo access
- **Demos** — audio samples organized by category and/or language
- **About / Bio** — personal introduction, background, studio specs
- **Services** — types of work offered (commercials, corporate, animation, etc.)
- **Clients / Credits** — logos of known brands worked with
- **Testimonials** — quotes from satisfied clients
- **Contact** — form, email, phone

Many sites are essentially **single-page or minimal-page designs**, especially smaller boutique solo-artist sites. Simpler is consistently praised — cluttered sites lose clients quickly.

---

## 2. The Homepage

The homepage is critical. Visitors make decisions in seconds. Key patterns:

### Hero Section
- Large, high-quality **professional headshot** (not a logo — a face creates connection)
- Short **tagline** communicating value, e.g. "Multilingual Voice Actor – English, German, French"
- Immediate access to a **demo reel** (play button above the fold)
- Primary **call-to-action** button: "Get a Quote", "Request an Audition", "Let's Chat"

### Below the Hero
- Featured audio demos (2–4 players for core categories)
- Client logo strip ("As heard on..." or "Trusted by...")
- Short bio snippet with link to full About page
- Testimonials section
- Contact shortcut

---

## 3. Audio Demos — The Most Critical Element

Every site agrees: **demos are the most important content**. No demo = no booking.

### Organisation
- Demos grouped by **content type**: Commercial, Corporate/Narration, Animation, Radio Imaging, E-Learning, IVR/Telephony, Promo
- For multilingual artists: demos further grouped or filtered by **language**
- Each demo clearly **labelled** with category and language

### Length & Format
- Individual demos: **60–90 seconds** each
- Demo reels show range — different tones, paces, styles
- Audio players should be **embedded directly on the page** (no downloading required)
- Some sites also include **video demos** (for TV work especially)

### Placement
- At minimum, featured demos on the homepage
- Dedicated Demos page with full categorized library

---

## 4. About / Bio Section

The bio serves to build trust and personality. Key conventions:

- Written in **first person**, warm and professional tone
- Covers: who you are, what you specialise in, languages spoken, markets served
- Mentions **notable clients or credits**
- Mentions **studio setup** (home studio with professional equipment builds confidence for remote clients)
- Optional: training background, career journey, personal touch (humanises the artist)
- Professional **headshots** throughout — not just one photo

---

## 5. Services Section

Multilingual and commercial-focused artists typically list services explicitly:

- Radio commercials
- TV commercials / broadcast
- Online / digital ads
- Corporate narration / internal comms
- E-learning
- IVR / on-hold telephony
- Animation / character
- Promos / trailers
- Documentary

For multilingual artists (highly relevant for Maud), services are often broken down **by language**: e.g. "French commercial voiceover", "German radio ad voiceover". This helps SEO and helps clients find the right talent immediately.

---

## 6. Trust Signals

Across all sites, trust signals are heavily used and clearly effective:

### Client Logos
- A "Trusted by" or "As heard on" logo strip is near-universal
- Shows recognisable brands (Mercedes, Amazon, BBC, etc.)
- Even one or two known brand logos dramatically increases credibility

### Testimonials
- 3–6 quotes from real clients, with name and job title
- Placed on homepage and/or dedicated page
- Short, specific, praising professionalism AND voice quality

### Credentials & Memberships
- Industry associations: Equity, BAFTA, SAG-AFTRA, One Voice Awards, etc.
- Award nominations or wins prominently displayed
- Years of experience stated clearly

### Studio Specifications
- Professional equipment listed (microphone model, DAW, acoustic treatment)
- Demonstrates broadcast-quality output capability
- Source-Connect or similar remote session tools mentioned

---

## 7. Calls to Action (CTAs)

CTAs are clear, repeated, and action-oriented. Common examples:

- "Get a Quote"
- "Request an Audition"
- "Book a Session"
- "Let's Chat"
- "Contact Me"
- "Request a Custom Sample"

Best practice: **one primary CTA** per section, consistent throughout the site. CTAs appear in: hero, after demos, after bio, in footer.

---

## 8. Contact Page

A dedicated, easy-to-find contact page is essential. Typical contents:

- **Contact form** (name, email, project type, message)
- **Direct email** address (always visible — don't hide behind forms only)
- Phone number (optional but builds trust)
- Response time promise (e.g. "I respond within 24 hours")
- Agent/management contact if represented
- Links to casting profiles (Voice123, Voices.com, etc.) if applicable

---

## 9. Multilingual Considerations

For artists working in multiple languages (very relevant for Maud — EN/FR/DE/IT):

### Language Switching
- Top-level navigation language selector (flag icons or text abbreviations)
- Each language version has its own translated content — not just translated demos
- SEO benefit: separate pages for each language and market

### Demo Organisation by Language
- Most multilingual artists present demos both by type AND by language
- A French client wants to find French commercial demos quickly
- Clear labelling: "Commercial – Français", "Radio – Deutsch", etc.

### Targeting Different Markets
- Different markets have different norms and expectations
- Copy on each language version should be written for that audience (not machine translated)
- Consider separate contact details or booking paths per market if relevant

---

## 10. Design Patterns & Aesthetics

### Dominant Styles
1. **Minimalist/Elegant** — dark or neutral palette, restrained typography, focus on the voice
2. **Warm/Personal** — softer tones, more personality, approachable
3. **Bold/Cinematic** — high contrast, dramatic, suits trailer/promo work

### What Works
- Clean, uncluttered layouts
- Professional photography (investment in good headshots pays off)
- Strong typographic hierarchy — clear headings, easy scanning
- Consistent colour scheme tied to personal brand
- Generous whitespace
- Fast load times (audio-heavy sites must be optimised)

### What to Avoid
- Auto-playing audio (widely disliked)
- Flash intros or excessive animation
- Poor mobile experience (significant % of clients browse on phone)
- Hard-to-find contact info
- Demos buried deep in navigation

---

## 11. Technical / SEO Notes

- **Mobile-first design** is essential — many clients browse on phones
- SEO-optimised page titles and meta descriptions per language
- Use keywords like "voiceover artist [language]", "radio commercial voice", "female voice actress [country]"
- Structured data (schema.org) can help with search visibility
- Fast audio loading — use optimised formats (MP3 at appropriate bitrate)
- Social media links (LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube) in footer

---

## 12. Pages Summary

Recommended minimum page set for a professional voiceover site:

| Page | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Home | First impression, hero, featured demos, trust signals, CTA |
| Demos | Full library of audio samples by category/language |
| About | Bio, headshots, studio specs, background |
| Services | What you do, for whom, in which languages |
| Clients | Brand logos, credits, testimonials |
| Contact | Form, email, response info |

Optional additions:
- Blog / News
- FAQ
- Rates / Pricing (controversial — many artists don't list prices publicly)

## 13. Notes Specific to Maud's Context

Based on research, these points are especially relevant:

1. **Multilingual capability (EN/FR/DE/IT) is a major differentiator** — feature it prominently from the first screen, not buried in the about page
2. **Radio and TV commercial specialisation** — clients in this space are busy agencies and producers; the site needs to be fast, professional, and demo-first
3. **European market** — European clients (particularly agencies in France, Germany, Switzerland, Italy) expect a very polished, understated design — less self-promotional than US sites
4. **Simple but effective** aligns well with the minimalist aesthetic popular among high-end European voiceover artists
5. **Demo quality matters more than quantity** — 3–5 excellent demos per language/category beats a sprawling demo library
6. **The contact flow must be frictionless** — radio/TV ad agencies are under deadline pressure; if contacting Maud takes more than 2 clicks, they'll move on
